ASRA expert practice recommendations: gastric point-of-care ultrasound to assess aspiration risk in medically complex patients

Reg Anesth Pain Med· Society recommendations·Jul 6 A guideline Free full text

ASRA-PM issues PRISMA-based expert recommendations for gastric POCUS to assess aspiration risk in medically complex patients — exactly the GLP-1 and comorbid population you block. It positions antral ultrasound as the risk-stratification tool where ASA fasting guidance (written for healthy patients) doesn't fit, letting you image rather than reflexively delay or secure the airway. For a community generalist doing regional, this is an actionable answer to the aspiration debate you can adopt now. Journal club will press on inter-rater reliability and where the qualitative "full stomach" threshold actually sits.

Regional anaesthesia and chronic postsurgical pain: a systematic review and network meta-analysis

Br J Anaesth· Network meta-analysis of RCTs·Jul 9 A NMA of RCTs

A PRISMA network meta-analysis asks whether your blocks do more than control acute pain — whether they reduce chronic postsurgical pain and long-term opioid use, and which techniques matter most. That reframes the regional-vs-none conversation from PACU comfort to a durable outcome your orthopedic patients care about. If the effect holds, it strengthens the case for a block in the populations where you currently decide case-by-case. Watch the between-trial heterogeneity and how loosely "regional" was pooled across very different techniques.

Remimazolam vs propofol for delirium in older patients recovering from lung resection: a randomised non-inferiority trial

Anaesthesia· RCT, non-inferiority, n=455·Jul 17 B single RCT

Remimazolam is spreading as a propofol alternative, but its benzodiazepine lineage revives the midazolam-delirium worry. This RCT tests non-inferiority for postoperative delirium in older patients — directly relevant as you weigh remimazolam for an aging general-OR population. A clean non-inferiority result removes a real hesitation; a signal toward more delirium is a reason to keep propofol in the elderly. Single-centre generalizability and the chosen non-inferiority margin are the questions to bring to journal club.
